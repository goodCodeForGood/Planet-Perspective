About Planet Perspective

Climate change is an urgent global crisis that requires universal awareness. The most apparent issue of which is rising greenhouse gases result in unprecedented temperature increases, extreme weather events, and rising sea levels to name a few. Whether we realize it or not, we are contributing to climate change as well through practices like overuse of single-use plastics and even forgetting switch of lights in the house. We are all passionate about spreading awareness of this topic as we believe our actions today directly affect our future.

Problem Statement

Many people are realizing that global warming is a huge issue, but most people are unaware of how their various actions contribute to the increase in global warming. Many people want to improve the situation but do not know how to get started and are also unaware of many other unusual solutions that can also better the conditions. So, our website provides awareness about the global warming / climate change issue in a heavily-interactive way through hands-on activities and practices that users can use to understand the issue and also actively fight climate change by inculcating our solutions in their life, real time.

Statistics

    Global Temperature Rise:

  • The Earth's average temperature has risen by about 1.2 degrees Celsius (2.2 degrees Fahrenheit) since the late 19th century.

    Greenhouse Gas Emissions:

  • Carbon dioxide (CO2) concentration in the atmosphere surpassed 415 parts per million (ppm) in 2021, a level not seen in millions of years.
  • The largest sources of global greenhouse gas emissions include the burning of fossil fuels for energy, deforestation, and industrial processes.

    Sea Level Rise:

  • Global sea levels have risen by about 8 inches (20 cm) over the past century.
  • Melting ice caps and glaciers contribute to rising sea levels.

    Extreme Weather Events:

  • There is an observed increase in the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, such as hurricanes, heatwaves, droughts, and heavy rainfall events.

    Arctic Ice Melt:

  • Arctic sea ice extent has been decreasing, with the Arctic warming at more than twice the global average rate.

    Loss of Biodiversity:

  • Climate change contributes to habitat loss and alteration, impacting ecosystems and leading to the loss of biodiversity.

    Ocean Acidification:

  • The oceans absorb about 30% of the carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere, leading to ocean acidification, which can harm marine life, particularly organisms with calcium carbonate shells.

    Renewable Energy Adoption:

  • The use of renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, has been increasing, but fossil fuels still dominate the global energy mix.

    International Agreements:

  • The Paris Agreement, adopted in 2015, aims to limit global warming to well below 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels, with efforts to limit it to 1.5 degrees Celsius.

It's essential to note that ongoing research and monitoring provide more detailed and updated information. Governments, scientists, and organizations around the world continue to work on mitigating and adapting to the impacts of climate change. For the latest information, refer to reputable sources like the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), the World Meteorological Organization (WMO), and other climate research institutions

Causes of Climate Change:

    Greenhouse Gas Emissions:

  • The burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas) for energy is a major contributor to the increase in greenhouse gases, particularly carbon dioxide (CO2), in the atmosphere.

    Deforestation:

  • The clearing of forests for agriculture, logging, and other purposes reduces the number of trees that absorb CO2 from the atmosphere.

    Industrial Processes:

  • Certain industrial activities release greenhouse gases, such as methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O), into the atmosphere.

    Agricultural Practices:

  • Agricultural activities, including livestock farming and rice cultivation, produce methane. The use of synthetic fertilizers also releases nitrous oxide.

    Waste Management:

  • Landfills and waste treatment processes can produce methane emissions.

    Land Use Changes:

  • Alterations in land use, such as urbanization and changes in land cover, can impact local and regional climates.

    Transportation:

  • The combustion of fossil fuels in vehicles and transportation systems contributes to CO2 emissions.

    Industrial Revolution:

  • The rapid increase in industrial activities during the 18th and 19th centuries marked a significant period of human-induced climate change.

Effects of Climate Change:

    Rising Temperatures:

  • Average global temperatures are increasing, leading to more frequent and intense heatwaves.

    Melting Ice and Rising Sea Levels:

  • Ice caps and glaciers are melting, contributing to rising sea levels and threatening coastal areas.

    Extreme Weather Events:

  • Increased frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, including hurricanes, droughts, floods, and wildfires.

    Ocean Acidification:

  • The absorption of excess CO2 by the oceans leads to acidification, harming marine life, particularly organisms with calcium carbonate shells.

    Changes in Precipitation Patterns:

  • Altered patterns of rainfall and snowfall, leading to more intense storms or longer periods of drought.

    Shifts in Ecosystems:

  • Changes in temperature and precipitation can disrupt ecosystems, affecting plant and animal species and their habitats.

    Loss of Biodiversity:

  • Climate change poses a threat to various species and ecosystems, leading to shifts in biodiversity and potential extinctions.

    Impacts on Agriculture:

  • Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns can affect crop yields and food production, leading to food insecurity.

    Health Risks:

  • Climate change can exacerbate health issues, including the spread of diseases, heat-related illnesses, and challenges in accessing clean water.

    Social and Economic Disruptions:

  • Climate-related events can lead to displacement, migration, and economic challenges, particularly in vulnerable communities.

Daily Life Suggestions for Climate Change

  • Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: Minimize waste by following the three Rs. Recycle paper, plastic, glass, and electronics.
  • Conserve Energy: Turn off lights and appliances when not in use. Use energy-efficient appliances and consider renewable energy sources.
  • Use Sustainable Transportation: Walk, bike, carpool, or use public transport to reduce carbon emissions from personal vehicles.
  • Conserve Water: Fix leaks, use water-efficient appliances, and collect rainwater for gardening.
  • Eat Sustainably: Choose local and seasonal foods to reduce the carbon footprint of food transportation. Reduce meat consumption and support sustainable farming practices.
  • Plant Trees: Trees absorb carbon dioxide and provide oxygen. Consider planting trees in your community or supporting reforestation projects.
  • Educate Others: Share information about climate change and sustainable practices with friends, family, and colleagues.
  • Minimize Single-Use Plastics: Opt for reusable items and reduce your reliance on single-use plastics to decrease environmental pollution.
  • Support Sustainable Brands: Choose products from companies committed to eco-friendly and ethical practices.
  • Conscious Water Usage: Be mindful of water consumption in daily activities, such as washing dishes efficiently and taking shorter showers.

Outreach Organizations

  • World Wildlife Fund (WWF): WWF works to protect the world's most vulnerable species and ecosystems. Impact: Conservation of endangered species, habitat preservation, and sustainable development.
  • 350.org: 350.org focuses on addressing climate change by reducing carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere. Impact: Advocacy for renewable energy, divestment from fossil fuels, and global climate awareness campaigns.
  • The Climate Reality Project: Founded by Al Gore, this organization educates and advocates for solutions to the climate crisis. Impact: Climate leadership training, public awareness campaigns, and policy advocacy for sustainable practices.
